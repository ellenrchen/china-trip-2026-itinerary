New Sides of HK

☁️ 80°F / 71°F · Partly cloudy with a chance of rain — light jacket in the evening
You land ~5AM. Take the Airport Express or cross-border coach to Shenzhen, check in, freshen up — then head back into HK via the MTR for a proper afternoon. Focus on Kowloon-side to keep travel efficient.
🗺️ What to See (Beyond the Peak)
  • Tsim Sha Tsui Waterfront — Stroll the Avenue of Stars along Victoria Harbour. The skyline views are just as good as the Peak, and it's flat. Go in the evening for the neon lights.
  • Mong Kok (旺角) — The most densely local neighbourhood in HK. Ladies' Market, Temple Street, Goldfish Market. Snack your way through stalls. Best around 7–10PM.
  • Sham Shui Po — Gritty, working-class, beloved by locals. Cheap dai pai dong stalls, fabric markets, old-school HK feel.
  • Temple Street Night Market — Classic night-market energy. Street food, fortune tellers, stalls. Peaks after 8PM.
🚇 Logistics

Cross into HK via Lo Wu or Lok Ma Chau MTR border crossing. Tsim Sha Tsui is a 20-minute ride from the border. Stay Kowloon-side — no need to cross the harbour. Head back to Shenzhen by midnight via the last MTR train (check border crossing hours).

Victoria Harbour from Tsim Sha Tsui
🍜 Must-Eat in Hong Kong
☕ HK Milk Tea + Toast
Silky pulled milk tea & condensed milk toast at a cha chaan teng. Try Mido Café in Yau Ma Tei.
🍜 Wonton Noodles
Delicate shrimp wontons in thin springy noodles & clear broth. A HK staple morning or night.
🧇 Egg Waffles (雞蛋仔)
Bubble waffles — crispy outside, soft inside. Modern versions add matcha or ice cream.
🦆 Roast Goose
Cantonese roast goose — crackling skin, juicy meat over rice. Yat Lok near Central is iconic.
🍢 Curry Fish Balls
Street stall classic — bouncy fish balls in a fragrant spiced curry sauce on a skewer.
🥭 Mango Pomelo Sago
Chilled dessert soup of mango, pomelo & sago in coconut milk. Perfect for the afternoon.

The Best of SZ

🌧️ 77°F / 68°F · Rain likely — Mar 31 is the rainiest day of the month here. Bring an umbrella.
🗺️ Things to Do
  • Dafen Oil Painting Village (大芬油画村) — An entire village of working artists and studios. Browse originals, commission a portrait, or just wander between the canvases. Completely unique to Shenzhen.
  • OCT LOFT (华侨城创意文化园) — Creative district built in repurposed factory spaces. Independent galleries, specialty coffee, boutiques. Great morning vibe.
  • Lianhua Mountain Park (莲花山公园) — Where locals do tai chi and fly kites. Easy walk, sweeping views of the city skyline. Relaxed and very local.
  • Huaqiangbei Electronics Market (华强北) — Even if you don't buy anything, walking through the world's largest electronics market is a spectacle. Great for phone accessories or anything tech.
📅 Suggested Day Flow

Morning: OCT LOFT for breakfast/coffee
Midday: Dafen Oil Painting Village
Afternoon: Huaqiangbei OR Lianhua Mountain
Evening: Local dai pai dong night food stalls

Dafen Oil Painting Village
🍽️ Must-Eat in Shenzhen
🍱 Chang Fen (肠粉)
Silky rice noodle rolls with shrimp or pork & sweet soy sauce. The quintessential Cantonese morning.
🍚 Claypot Rice (煲仔饭)
Rice with lap cheong & mushrooms in a clay pot; the crispy bottom crust is the best bite.
🦆 Roast Goose & Squab
Even better than HK. Look for 石岐乳鸽 (Shiqi-style squab) while in Guangdong.
🍲 Seafood Hot Pot
Light, sweet fish-bone broth with fresh shrimp, crab & clam. Not spicy — pure seafood flavor.
🍮 Sugar Water (糖水)
Sweet soup dessert — lotus seed, longan, or red bean. Hot or iced. The Cantonese answer to dessert.
🥟 Dim Sum
Siu mai, har gow, cheung fun — order early at a local teahouse, never a chain.

Two Days in Xiamen

🗓️ Day 1 (Apr 1) — Coastal Hotel Zone
⛅ 72°F / 60°F · Mild and pleasant — occasional showers possible, umbrella handy
Arrival
High-speed train from Shenzhen North → Xiamen North (~3.5 hrs). Check in to the coastal hotel. Drop bags, settle in.
Afternoon
Gulangyu Island (鼓浪屿) — take the short ferry. No cars, gorgeous colonial-era buildings, Piano Museum, tree-lined lanes. Right by the hotel zone — easy to reach.
Evening
Seafood dinner along the coast near the hotel — fresh clams, crab, shrimp. Xiamen's coastal restaurants are at their best at night.
🗓️ Day 2 (Apr 2) — Explore the City
🌦️ 72°F / 59°F · Coolest day in Xiamen — light layer + umbrella, showers likely
Morning
Slow morning at the coastal hotel. Breakfast, then head out to explore.
Midday
Xiamen University — one of China's most beautiful campuses. Walk Furong Lake and the "Love Tunnel" (芙蓉隧道), walls covered in student murals. Nanputuo Temple (南普陀寺) is right next door.
Afternoon
Zhongshan Road (中山路) — Xiamen's famous pedestrian street. Old Amoy shophouse architecture, snack shops, local life.
Evening
Zeng Cuo'an (曾厝垵) — former fishing village turned creative quarter. Street food, cafés, great atmosphere after dark. Early night — early flight to Shanghai tomorrow morning.
✈️ Apr 3: Fly Xiamen → Shanghai

Morning flight from Xiamen Gaoqi Airport (XMN) → Shanghai Pudong (PVG) or Hongqiao (SHA). Flight is ~2 hours — book early for good prices. Aim to land by midday to leave enough time for mom's health checkups.

Xiamen University — Furong Lake
🍜 Must-Eat in Xiamen
🍜 Satay Noodles (沙茶面)
Xiamen's signature dish. Rich satay-spiced broth, alkaline noodles & toppings. Salty, smoky & addictive.
🦪 Oyster Omelette (蚵仔煎)
Crispy egg omelette loaded with fresh oysters and sweet potato starch. Get it from a stall.
🥜 Peanut Soup (花生汤)
Slow-simmered until silky. "Rotten but not mushy, sweet but not greasy" — a local classic.
🌊 Fresh Seafood
It's a port city. Clams, crab, shrimp — order at any seafood restaurant and let them cook it simply.
🍬 Tusu Bing (土笋冻 & 土酥饼)
Flaky local pastry cakes with sweet fillings. Great as a snack walking Zhongshan Road.
🐛 Sandworm Jelly (土笋冻)
For the adventurous — jelly made from marine worms. Surprisingly refreshing with vinegar and chili.

Shanghai

🗓️ Day 1 (Apr 3) — Health Checkups Day
🧥 61°F / 47°F · Noticeably cooler than Xiamen — pack a real jacket. Overcast.
Arrival
Morning flight from Xiamen — land midday. Check in to Shanghai hotel, drop bags.
Midday
Mom's health checkups. Consider a hotel near Xuhui or Jing'an for easy hospital access.
Afternoon
French Concession (法租界) — if there's energy. Wukang Road is a gentle, beautiful walk: plane trees, art deco buildings, good cafés. Very low effort, very high reward.
Evening
Dinner at Jia Jia Tang Bao (佳家汤包) on Huanghe Road — one of Shanghai's best xiaolongbao spots. Cash only, worth any queue.
🗓️ Day 2 (Apr 4) — The Big Day
⛅ 61°F / 53°F · Cool and partly cloudy — light rain possible. Layers for the evening.
Morning
Wukang Road (武康路) — Start slow in the French Concession. Plane tree canopy, art deco villas, great cafés. Perfect for a morning stroll and a proper coffee.
Afternoon
Gentle Monster HAUS Shanghai Robotic Art ✓ — kinetic giant robotic heads and AI-like face sculptures. Free to walk through. 798–812 Huaihai Zhong Lu, Huangpu.
Evening
Yu Garden + Huxin Pavilion (豫园 + 湖心亭) Red Pavilion ✓ — even better at night. The red-lacquered pavilion and zigzag bridge glow with lantern light, and the Chenghuangmiao Bazaar (城隍庙) comes fully alive after dark — street food, souvenirs, incredible atmosphere.
Night
The Bund (外滩) — just a 10-minute walk from Yu Garden. End the night with the full Pudong skyline glittering across the water. One of the great city views on earth.

Yu Garden — Huxin Pavilion (湖心亭) · The Red Pavilion
🏯 Shanghai Highlights Summary
  • Huxin Pavilion (湖心亭) Red Pavilion
    The 240-year-old mid-lake teahouse at Yu Garden — even more magical at night when it's lit up with lanterns. Red-painted wood, upturned eaves, zigzag bridge. Freshly restored in 2025. Come after dark.
  • Gentle Monster HAUS Shanghai Robotic Art
    The iconic eyewear store turned art space — kinetic giant heads, robotic face sculptures, and surreal installations that rotate regularly. Free entry. 798–812 Huaihai Zhong Lu.
  • The Bund (外滩)
    A must. The 1920s colonial waterfront facing the Pudong skyscrapers at night is unmissable. Walk both directions along the promenade.
  • Wukang Road (武康路)
    The French Concession's best street. Plane tree canopy, art deco villas, independent boutiques, great for photos and a slow afternoon coffee.
🍽️ Must-Eat in Shanghai
🥟 Xiaolongbao (小笼包)
Soup dumplings — go to Jia Jia Tang Bao (佳家汤包) or Nan Xiang Steamed Bun on Yuyuan Rd.
🥘 Shengjianbao (生煎包)
Pan-fried pork buns with crispy bottoms & a burst of broth. Try Yang's Fry-Dumpling (小杨生煎).
🍝 Scallion Oil Noodles
Noodles with caramelized scallion oil & soy. Deceptively simple, deeply addictive.
🥩 Red Braised Pork (红烧肉)
Melt-in-your-mouth pork belly slow-braised in sweet soy sauce. Shanghai's signature dish.
🦀 Drunken Crab (醉蟹)
Hairy crab marinated in Shaoxing wine & spices. April is just after peak season but still a good call.
🧆 Scallion Pancakes (葱油饼)
Crispy, flaky, fragrant — the ultimate Shanghai street snack. Fresh from a street stall — beats everything.
